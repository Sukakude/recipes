Whiskey Burger

Home

Whiskey Burgers are hamburger patties that are soaked in whiskey and garlic. Once you try them you may never go back to a boring, old, plain burger again!

Ingredients

  • (4) 6-6½ oz. hamburger patties
  • ¼ c. whiskey
  • 3 garlic cloves, chopped
  • 8 strips of bacon
  • 8 thick-sliced pieces of cheddar cheese
  • 2 large vidalia onions, peeled and chopped
  • 2 T. olive oil
  • 2 T. reserved bacon fat
  • 1 t. salt
  • 4 hamburger buns
  • Lettuce, tomatoes, or your favorite hamburger fixings

Steps

  1. Place the bacon in a large skillet and cook until crisp. Remove to a paper towel lined plate to cool. Reserve 2 T. of the bacon fat.
  2. Discard the remaining bacon fat and wipe out the skillet.
  3. Add the olive oil and the 2 T. of bacon fat to the same skillet, then add the onions, cooking over low heat.
  4. Add the salt and cook, turning the onions often for 30-40 minutes until they are brown and caramelized. Set aside to cool. The onions take a while to cook down and get sweet, about 30-40 minutes over low heat. Don’t rush them. Burning or scorching the onions will make them bitter. Take your time and you’ll reap the sweet rewards.
  5. While the onions are cooking, add the whiskey and chopped garlic to a shallow dish. ( I use a microplane grater to chop my garlic.) Put in the hamburger patties and let marinate for 15 minutes. Then flip and marinate another 15 on the other side.
  6. Grill the burgers over medium-high heat for 5 minutes per side for a medium/medium rare burger. If you aren’t using 6 or 6½ oz. hamburgers you might want to cook the hamburgers less if you’re looking for a medium rare burger.
  7. The last minute of grilling, add two slices of cheese to each burger and close the lid to melt the cheese.
  8. Remove the hamburgers to a platter.
  9. Place each hamburger on a bottom bun, then top with a large spoonful of caramelized onions and then one or two strips of bacon broken in half.
  10. Top each burger with the top bun and serve.

Enjoy!
